A NEW JOURNEY

You might be wondering why I'm looking for a data science job when I was a perfectly competent sysadmin. The short answer is that I enjoy it more. More specifically, I have learned that I'm attracted to complicated rulesets and systems, which is why I enjoy complex games and why my favorite part of system administration was designing and implementing new projects. Data science, and especially machine learning, allows me to scratch the same itch with a few major advantages:

  • Data science is about utilizing data to discover and find value in new knowledge.
    • System Administration is mostly the art of making disparate products made by different companies work together.
  • Data science can generate revenue for the company.
    • System administration is always overhead.
  • Data scientists must collaborate, communicate, and learn as much as possible about the business.
    • No one thinks about the sysadmins unless something breaks, which means they are incentivized to avoid notice. This means that data science is better for my emotional health.
  • Data science still has a need for The Troubleshooter, and that's me.
    • From my first teenage job at McDonald's, through my career as a sysadmin, and into grad school, I have consistently developed a reputation for successfully handling unusual problems.
  • Data science is more complex than anything I have done before.
    • This makes me feel like I'm progressing and improving as a human.
  • Data science provides tools to increase my personal understanding of the world.
    • System administrators also require continuous learning, but the new knowledge tends to be limited to in-depth information about specific computer programs.
  • Data science allows for and rewards creative solutions to the problems it poses.
    • System administration requires rigid adherence to established best practices in order to demonstrate due diligence when something inevitably goes wrong.
  • Data science can still utilize my previous experience as domain knowledge.
    • I've gotten a few years out of date while pursuing my degree, but the underlying concepts haven't changed.
